My trip to Ecuador and the Galapagos Islands!

Quito

Our trip began with a stop in Quito, the capital of Ecuador.

The highlight of Quito was traveling up a volcano in a gondola.

Cuenca

Cuenca is a small town in southern Ecuador's Andes mountains.

It's famous for it's volcanic spas and being the birthplace of Panama hats.

Guayacil

Guayacil is a coastal city in western Ecuador.

It's known as the gateway to the Galapagos Islands.

The Galapagos Islands

The Galapagos Islands are a series of volcanic islands about 600 miles west of Ecuador.

They are well known for being the birthplace of Charles Darwin's theory of evolution.

The best part of our trip to the islands was all of the magnificent wildlife.
We spent a week exploring the islands and got up close and personal with many of the islands unusual species.
Including:

  • The famous Galapagos tortoise
  • Marine iguanas
  • Sea lions
  • Marine turtles
  • Manta, eagle, golden, and sting rays
  • Sea horses
  • Penguins
  • Blue footed boobies
  • And my favorite, black and white tipped reef sharks!
